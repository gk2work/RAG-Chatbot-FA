Studying a master’s program in Barcelona proffers a unique blend of academic excellence, cultural immersion, and career opportunities.
Barcelona has a total student population of almost 200,000 students, with one tenth of this is international students, a significant population
include students in Bachelor and Master Programs in Barcelona. Apart from numerous premium institutes such as C3S Business School, which
focus largely on Bachelor and Master Program in Barcelona, the Barcelona city has five public and six private universities, including the
University of Barcelona.
Why you should study a master’s program in Barcelona specifically
Studying a master’s program in Barcelona offers a multitude of benefits, making it an attractive choice for students seeking an enriching
academic experience. Barcelona is home to renowned universities and institutions offering high-quality master’s programs across various
disciplines. Institutions such as C3S Business School, the University of Barcelona, Pompeu Fabra University, and ESADE Business School
consistently rank among the top in Europe, ensuring a rigorous academic experience and valuable credentials. Barcelona’s rich history, vibrant
arts scene, and diverse cultural heritage provide an unparalleled backdrop for academic pursuits while studying in Barcelona. Studying in
Barcelona offers the opportunity to immerse yourself in Catalan culture, explore iconic landmarks like the Sagrada Familia and Park Güell, and
engage with a dynamic community of locals and international students.
Affordability
Barcelona is generally considered to be an affordable city for students studying in Spain compared to other major European cities like London
or Paris or cities in Switzerland, although the cost of living can vary depending on individual lifestyle choices and preferences. Renting a room
in a shared apartment (known as a piso compartido) is a common and relatively affordable option for students studying in Barcelona. The cost
of accommodation can vary depending on the neighborhood and proximity to the city center, and these two criteria the C3S Business School
certainly qualifies to be a preferred choice. Gràcia, Eixample, and Poble Sec are popular areas among students, as C3S Business School
students’ always testify to this, due to their vibrant atmosphere and relatively lower rent prices compared to the city center.
3 | This is how the Master’s Program in Barcelona unfolds for you
Daily expenses such as groceries, transportation, dining out, and entertainment are generally reasonable in Barcelona.
Students can save money by shopping at local markets, cooking at home, and taking advantage of student discounts
offered at many establishments.
Barcelona has an efficient public transportation system including buses, metro, and trains, which makes it easy and
affordable to get around the city. Students under 25 years old are eligible for discounted transportation passes, further
reducing the cost of commuting.

FAQs for Students Considering the PhD by
 Portfolio Programme at C3S Business School
 How to Apply for a PhD by Portfolio?
 You apply with C3S Business School using the standard process required by the awarding body to
 enrol, similar to the traditional route of a PhD. Additionally, you will need to submit a portfolio of your
 prior publication track record and a supporting statement outlining the work of these existing
 publications, demonstrating how they relate to the subject area you’re applying for. A formal PhD
 research proposal is not required as the majority of the research should already have been
 completed.
 If your application is successful, you will be assigned one or more supervisors who will advise you on
 which publications should form your final submission and how to write your commentary. This
 commentary will describe the aims of your research, include an analytical discussion of the main
 results, and contextualise your work.
 What happens after I’ve applied?
 Yes, you will have a primary supervisor similar to traditional PhD students. The supervisor's role is to
 help establish a clear narrative for your publications, providing critical appraisal when necessary.
 They will guide you on how to structure the required introductory and concluding sections before
 submitting your portfolio for examination.
 As a PhD candidate at C3S, you will have access to a professional development programme provided
 by the Research Development Unit, which includes workshops, mentoring, and coaching. Our
 dedicated Careers and Employability team will assist you in preparing for your career after
 completing your PhD.
 How does Assessment Work?
 Your submitted body of work will be assessed by two examiners who are experts in your research
 area, followed by a viva examination, in line with conventional PhD practices. You must demonstrate
 that your work has made an original contribution to the field to be awarded the degree.
 26
What kind of publications can I use in my Portfolio?
 Typically, you can include 5 publications in your submission to the PhD examiners, but this can vary
 from 3 to 10 depending on specific circumstances. Publications may consist of peer-reviewed journal
 articles, published book chapters, or technical reports.
 How many papers should I publish?
 There’s no definitive answer. The focus should be on making an original and significant contribution
 to your field. An Australian survey found that the average number of papers included in these was
 4.5, ranging from 1 to 12. Quality is often more important than quantity.
 Should I co-author with my supervisor?
 Co-authoring with supervisors can provide valuable experience in writing journal articles and
 collaborating in research teams. Establish clear expectations between you and your supervisor early
 on to ensure a smooth working relationship.
 What if my paper gets rejected?
 Rejection is a normal part of the academic process. It's essential to understand that a rejection
 doesn't necessarily reflect the quality of your work but rather its fit for the specific journal at that
 time.
 What are the course and seminar milestone requirements?
 PhD students are encouraged to present at least two talks on their research and attend a minimum of
 five research seminars and a one-day research symposium each year. The Annual Graduate Student
 Conference typically fulfils these requirements.
 Why do you want to do a doctoral programme?
 Consider your motivations carefully. If you aim to improve future career prospects
 or pursue a passion for research, these motivations can provide the focus you
 need to succeed in your doctoral studies.

Anny Z’s Workation to Tanzania: Key
 Insights from Dar es Salaam 
In August 2024, Anny Z from C3S Business School visited Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, uncovering essential details about student
 enrollment and visa processes that are crucial for prospective students and their sponsors.
 Sponsorship and Financial Proof
 For students from Tanzania, Rwanda, and Burundi, sponsorship is
 limited to parents, the students themselves, or older siblings.
 Sponsors are advised to maintain a bank balance between €12,000
 and €14,000. It has been observed that demonstrating a slightly
 higher amount than the standard Income and Financial Proof
 Minimum (IPRAM) of €10,000 for Spain often results in better visa
 approval rates.
 Appointment Scheduling and Educational Gaps
 Given the high volume of applications, agents and students must
 notify the authorities at least one week before scheduling an
 appointment. This advance notice ensures that all necessary
 admission documents can be prepared and submitted to the
 embassy in time.
 Students with educational gaps exceeding two years need to
 provide official documentation explaining these gaps to clarify
 their academic history.
 Ms. Anny Zade (Regional Manager)
 C3S Business School, Carrer de Londres 6, 
Porta 9, 08029 Barcelona, Spain
 Telephone: +34 931 168 821  (Ext: 123)
 Admission Process and Tuition Payment
 Students should clearly outline their educational objectives and
 demonstrate financial stability during admission interviews. An
 initial payment amounting to around 60% of the total tuition
 fees, which range from €6,500 to €9,000, is required. This
 upfront payment helps show serious intent and positively
 influences visa approval.
 Document Review
 The embassy will review all submitted documents and may
 request additional information within 10 working days if needed.
 There might also be a personal interview, depending on the case.
 For detailed guidelines and required documents, please refer to
 the Embassy Checklist.

 From July 8th to July 15th, I had the delightful opportunity to escape to Tunisia for a

 bachelor’s programs, they’re not shelling out more than 8,000 euros a year. Lucky
 for us, C3S fits snugly within this budget, making our Business Management and
 Business Computing programs particularly attractive.

Lessons from the Final:
 FOR THE WINNING TEAM (SPAIN):
 Resilience & Adaptability:
 Spain demonstrated exceptional resilience, particularly in their
 quarter-final against Germany. Their ability to adapt their
 game plan in the final, focusing on controlling the midfield, was
 key to their victory
 Team Cohesion:
 The Spanish team displayed strong unity, with players like
 Rodri and Dani Olmo leading by example. Their chemistry on
 the pitch was crucial in overcoming England’s aggressive style
 of play.
 FOR THE LOSING TEAM (ENGLAND):
 Handling Pressure:
 England reached their second consecutive Euro final,
 but once again fell short. The lesson here is the importance of
 mental toughness and handling the pressure of big matches.
 Strategic Flexibility:
 England’s strategy was effective throughout the tournament
 but lacked flexibility in the final. A more adaptable approach
 could have countered Spain’s midfield dominance.
 MANAGEMENT LESSONS FROM EURO 2024:
 Importance of Leadership - Rodri’s Leadership: Spain’s success was largely due to effective leadership on and off the field. In a business
 context, strong leadership can guide a team through challenges and towards victory.
 Adaptation to Changing Conditions - Game Plan Adjustments: Spain’s ability to adjust their strategy during the final highlights the
 importance of adaptability in management. Companies must be agile and ready to shift tactics when facing unforeseen challenges.
 Team Dynamics - Spain’s Cohesion vs. England’s Pressure: The importance of a cohesive team cannot be overstated. In business,
 fostering a strong team spirit can lead to greater success, whereas an inability to manage internal pressure can lead to missed
 opportunities.
 Learning from Failure - England’s Near Miss: England’s loss, while disappointing, offers valuable lessons. In business, setbacks should
 be viewed as opportunities for learning and growth, paving the way for future success.
 Conclusion: UEFA Euro 2024 was not just a showcase of footballing talent, but also a source of valuable 
lessons in leadership, adaptability, and team dynamics. Both Spain’s victory and England’s near miss offer 
insights that can be applied far beyond the pitch. Whether in sports or business, success often hinges on
 how well teams can lead, adapt, and learn from both their triumphs and their defeats.

 As C3S Business School celebrates its 10th anniversary, it is not merely a reflection on a decade of achievements but a heartfelt
 tribute to the transformative journey of countless students who have realized their dreams through higher education. Our story is a
 testament to the power of education in changing lives and creating futures.
 A Journey Marked by Vision and Resilience
 C3S began its journey in 2014 in the serene town of Castelldefels, with a vision to provide more than just academic knowledge. It was
 about creating a nurturing environment where dreams could flourish. The move to Barcelona in 2017, with a team of 25 dedicated
 professionals, marked a significant step forward, opening new doors for students and expanding our horizons.
 In 2018, our global aspirations took shape as we formed partnerships with universities in the USA, Poland, Bulgaria, and Italy. This
 expansion was not just a strategic move but a commitment to providing students with a world-class education that bridges continents
 and cultures. The opening of an offshore office in 2019 symbolized our growing global presence, reflecting our dedication to reaching
 students wherever they are.
 The year 2020 was a period of profound change and adaptation. Relocating to a modern facility in Barcelona was not just a physical
 move but a metaphor for the new possibilities and opportunities that awaited our students. The achievement of Google certification
 was more than a credential—it was a pledge to embrace technological advancements that enhance learning and empower students.
 Despite the challenges of 2021, C3S continued to fulfill its mission. Partnering with UCAM University and achieving a 100% internship
 placement rate underscored our commitment to ensuring that every student has the opportunity to succeed. The alliance with a
 prestigious UK university in 2022 further expanded our reach, offering new avenues for students to pursue their passions.
 By 2023, C3S had become a vibrant community of over 45 nationalities, each student contributing to the rich tapestry of experiences
 and perspectives that define our school. This diversity is not just a statistic but a reflection of our core belief that education should be
 a universal bridge to dreams and opportunities.

OVERVIEW OBJECTIVES
 The objective of the OTHM Level 4 Diploma in Business Management qualification
 is to provide learners with an excellent foundation for building a career in a range
 of organisations. It is designed to ensure each learner is ‘business ready’: a
 confident, independent thinker with a detailed knowledge of business and
 management and equipped with the skills to adapt rapidly to change.
 The qualification will provide learners with the underpinning knowledge,
 understanding and skills associated with the business activities, policies,
 procedures and processes required to work effectively in a business environment.
 The units combine both theoretical and practical knowledge in the areas of
 academic and research skills, business and global operations, finance and
 accounting, business communication, leadership and team working.
 Successful completion of this qualification will support learners progressing to
 university, and provide learners with the requisite skills and knowledge to enter
 the world of work in their chosen sector.

Exploring Strategic Rivalry at C3S Business School
 Barcelona: A Comparative Analysis of Pepsi and
 Coca-Cola
 On November 5th, 2024, C3S Business School Barcelona
 hosted an insightful workshop titled Comparative Analysis of
 Two Giants: Pepsi and Coca-Cola. This event, which took
 place from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM, provided students with a
 unique opportunity to analyze the long-standing rivalry
 between these two global beverage brands. As a leading
 institution focused on preparing future business leaders,
 C3S Business School consistently delivers relevant, hands
on learning experiences, and this workshop was a prime
 example of how the school fosters strategic thinking and
 real-world business acumen.
 Unlocking Business
 Strategies: 
Pepsi vs. Coca-Cola
 In the competitive world of global branding, few rivalries
 are as iconic as Pepsi vs. Coca-Cola. These brands are
 not just competitors; they represent distinct business
 strategies that have shaped the marketplace for
 decades. This workshop aimed to dissect their individual
 approaches to business strategy, examining both their
 similarities and differences to highlight key lessons in
 corporate strategy. Students had the chance to explore
 the powerful marketing tactics and branding decisions
 that have positioned these companies as household
 names across the globe.
 Both Pepsi and Coca-Cola boast impressive global
 footprints and customer loyalty, but they take
 fundamentally different approaches to their branding.
 Coca-Cola leans heavily into emotional branding, often
 invoking a sense of nostalgia and community in its
 marketing campaigns. In contrast, Pepsi has positioned
 itself as a youthful, trend-forward brand, frequently
 aligning itself with pop culture and innovative marketing
 strategies. This distinction formed the basis of the
 workshop’s discussions, offering students a
 comprehensive look at how two brands can coexist in
 the same market while cultivating unique identities.

 One of the primary lessons from the workshop was
 the importance of strategic positioning. Even though
 Pepsi and Coca-Cola operate in the same industry,

 students aspiring to craft their own business
 strategies in the future.
 Adapting to market trends was another key

 willingness to embrace innovation, offering students
 valuable insights into how brands can evolve in
 response to changing consumer preferences.
 Perhaps most fascinating was the focus on consumer perception. Subtle differences in branding,
 tone, and marketing messages can have a profound impact on consumer loyalty and choice. By
 comparing the two brands, students learned how small nuances in marketing can drive success in
 a highly competitive industry.

The Significance of 
C3S Business School’s
 AI Research for
 Students (JASSS)
 C3S Business School’s research study, "Qualitative Methods in Statistics
 and Methodology: Artificial Intelligence (AI)," was conducted by esteemed
 researchers Aida Mehrad, Mohammad Hossein Tahriri Zangeneh, Meriem
 Bouzedif, Neema George Rweramila, and Hamza Boutament. Their work,
 published in the Journal of Advanced Studies in Social Sciences, delves
 into the critical role of artificial intelligence (AI) in enhancing qualitative
 research. By acknowledging the value of human oversight alongside AI
 tools, the researchers set a precedent for innovative and ethical academic
 methodologies.
 JASSS is a prestigious, peer-reviewed academic journal managed by the
 BAHISEEN Institute for Research & Digital Transformation, Islamabad. It
 publishes high-quality research across various social science disciplines,
 fostering rigorous scholarship and critical dialogue. The journal provides a
 platform for original research that contributes to both the theoretical and
 empirical advancement of social science knowledge, engaging scholars,
 practitioners, and students in discussions that bridge academic research
 with real-world application. JASSS is dedicated to exploring innovative
 methodologies, interdisciplinary approaches, and the use of technology to
 enhance research, making it an ideal forum for this important work on AI and
 qualitative research.
 Why This Research is
 Crucial
 The team of researchers highlights the growing
 importance of AI in addressing the challenges
 posed by traditional qualitative research methods.
 Their work demonstrates how qualitative methods,
 essential for exploring social complexities such as
 diversity and globalization, can be amplified using
 AI tools like ChatGPT.

semana después del comienzo de las clases, no se dará ningún reembolso. La aprobación de los reembolsos debido a circunstancias 
excepcionales queda a discreción de la administración de la escuela y se basaría en cada caso. Se cobrará una tarifa de

cuenta que, en este caso, todos los documentos originales previamente expedidos a los estudiantes por la escuela (incluidas las 
cartas de visa) deben devolverse a la C3S. 
7. Casos de denegación de visa 
7.1. La escuela proporciona servicios educativos y NO son asesores de inmigración. La escuela no está regulada para ofrecer 
ningún consejo de inmigración. Si necesita asesoramiento sobre inmigración, debe buscar asesoramiento legal independiente, 
ya que la Escuela no puede y no ofrece ningún asesoramiento sobre inmigración. 
7.2. La Escuela y sus empleados no son asesores de inmigración y, por lo tanto, la responsabilidad de la validez y precisión de una 
solicitud de VISA recae exclusivamente en el estudiante. Tenga en cuenta que es responsabilidad exclusiva del estudiante 
asegurarse de que cumple con los requisitos de inmigración. 
7.3. La escuela no puede ni será responsable de ningún rechazo de VISA basado en razones que estén dentro del control razonable 
del estudiante, incluido el hecho de que el estudiante no proporcione los documentos necesarios a la embajada o C3S, no demuestre 
el apoyo financiero adecuado a la embajada o C3S, presente una solicitud incompleta, declaraciones falsas o falsificación de 
documentos. El estudiante es responsable si la solicitud de VISA se ha realizado tarde y su inscripción en C3S ya ha sido 
procesada. Habrá una deducción adicional en el reembolso o no habrá reembolso según las pautas de C3S.

de estas fallas declaradas por el artículo 39.5.b. del Real Decreto 557/2011 “La visa será denegada cuando, en apoyo de la petición, 
se hayan presentado documentos falsos o se hayan presentado denuncias inexactas, o de mala fe” existe el derecho a un reembolso

7. Visa Refusal Cases 
7.1. The School provides educational services and are NOT immigration advisers. The School is not regulated to offer any immigration 
advice. If you require immigration advice, you should seek independent legal advice as the School cannot and do not offer any 
immigration advice. 
7.2. The School and its employees are not immigration advisers and therefore the responsibility for the validity and accuracy of a VISA 
application lies solely with the student. Please note that it is the student’s sole responsibility to ensure that they meet immigration 
requirements. 
7.3. The School cannot and will not be held responsible for any VISA refusal based on reasons which are within the student’s 
reasonable control including, the student failing to provide the necessary documents to embassy or C3S, failing to demonstrate 
adequate financial support to embassy or C3S, submitting an incomplete application, false representations being made, or forgery of 
documents. The student is held responsible if the VISA application has been made late and their C3S enrolment has already been 
processed. There will be additional deduction in refund or no refund as per C3S guidelines. 
7.4. If a student’s VISA application is refused due to a fault attributable to the student such as, but not limited to, one of these stated 
faults by the article 39.5.b. of Royal Decree 557/2011 : 
“The visa will be denied when, in support of the petition, false documents or inaccurate allegations have been presented, or act in a not 
goodwill.”
